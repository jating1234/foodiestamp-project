Interesting Foodie Facts

1. Did you know that honey never spoils? Archaeologists have found pots of honey in ancient Egyptian tombs that are over 3,000 years old and still perfectly edible!

2. The world’s most expensive pizza costs over $12,000 and is topped with ingredients like edible gold, lobster, and caviar.

3. Chocolate was once used as currency by the Aztecs. They valued cacao beans so highly that they used them to trade goods and services.

4. Apples float in water because 25% of their volume is air.

5. A single coffee tree yields only about one pound of coffee per year.

6. French fries are not actually French. They originated in Belgium!

7. Tomatoes were once considered poisonous: In the 18th century, Europeans thought tomatoes were toxic and called them "poison apples".

8. Carrots were originally purple: Orange carrots only became popular in the 17th century due to Dutch agricultural practices.

9. Pineapples take a long time to grow: A single pineapple can take up to 2 years to fully mature.

10. Popsicles were invented by accident: In 1905, an 11-year-old boy left a mixture of soda and water outside on a cold night, creating the first popsicle.

11. Cheese is the most stolen food in the world: Approximately 4% of all cheese produced worldwide ends up being stolen.

12. Peppers can trick your brain: The heat from chili peppers is not a taste but a pain response triggered by capsaicin.

13. Potatoes were the first food grown in space: NASA and the University of Wisconsin partnered to grow potatoes in space in 1995.

14. Almonds are seeds, not nuts: Technically, almonds are the seeds of a fruit related to peaches.

15. The largest pizza ever made: In 2012, a team in Rome created a pizza measuring over 13,500 square feet.

16. Watermelons are 92% water: That's why they’re a great hydrating snack during hot weather.

17. The sandwich is named after a person: The Earl of Sandwich asked for meat between slices of bread so he could eat without interrupting his card game.

18. Cucumber is a fruit: Despite being used as a vegetable in cooking, cucumbers are fruits by botanical definition.

19. Honey is a natural energy booster: It contains natural sugars and carbohydrates, perfect for quick energy replenishment.

20. Arachibutyrophobia is the fear of peanut butter sticking to the roof of your mouth: Yes, it’s a real thing!

21. Bananas are berries, but strawberries aren't: Botanically, bananas meet the criteria of a berry, while strawberries do not.
